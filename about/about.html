üìã Privacy Policies

Required by GDPR, CCPA, and virtually every data protection law worldwide. Privacy policies explain how a website collects, uses, and protects your personal information. Without one, sites face fines up to ‚Ç¨20 million or 4% of global revenue under GDPR.

Learn more: GDPR Privacy Notice Guide ICO Guide
üö´ Do Not Sell (CCPA)

Required by California's CCPA/CPRA for businesses that sell personal information. California residents have the right to opt-out of the sale of their data. Similar laws now exist in Virginia, Colorado, Connecticut, and other states.

Learn more: California CCPA CA Privacy Agency
üì• Data Subject Request Optional

GDPR and CCPA grant users the right to access, download, or delete their personal data. Many sites provide dedicated forms or portals for submitting Data Subject Access Requests (DSARs).

üç™ Cookie Compliance

üç™ Cookie Banners

Mandated by the EU ePrivacy Directive and GDPR for any site using non-essential cookies. Users must give informed consent before tracking cookies are placed. Non-compliance can result in significant penalties from data protection authorities.

Learn more: GDPR Cookie Guide EU Cookie Rules
üìÑ Cookie Policy

A detailed document explaining what cookies are used, their purposes, and retention periods. Required alongside cookie banners to provide full transparency under GDPR's informed consent requirements.

Learn more: ICO Cookie Guidance
‚öôÔ∏è Cookie Settings

GDPR and ePrivacy require that consent be as easy to withdraw as it is to give. Cookie preference centers allow users to manage their consent choices at any time, not just when the banner first appears.

Learn more: EDPB Consent Guidelines

üìú Terms of Service

The legal contract between the website and its users. Terms of Service define acceptable use, liability limitations, and user rights. While not legally mandated, they're essential for protecting both the business and establishing clear user expectations.

Learn more: FTC Business Guide
‚öñÔ∏è Legal Notices / Impressum

Required in many jurisdictions including Germany (Impressum) and other EU countries. Legal notices identify the website operator, provide contact information, and may include business registration details and VAT numbers.

ü§ù Dispute Resolution Optional

Often required for e-commerce sites under EU ODR regulations. Must inform consumers about available dispute resolution mechanisms and link to the EU Online Dispute Resolution platform for cross-border transactions.

Learn more: EU ODR Platform Out-of-Court Resolution
üìû Contact Information Optional

Required by various e-commerce laws (EU Consumer Rights Directive) and helps establish legitimacy. Users should be able to easily find how to contact the business.

Learn more: EU Consumer Rights

üõí Consumer Protection

üí∞ Refund / Return Policy Optional

Required for e-commerce in most jurisdictions (FTC in US, EU Consumer Rights Directive). Clearly explains how customers can return products and receive refunds. Essential for building consumer trust.

Learn more: FTC Mail Order Rule EU Returns
üì¶ Shipping Policy Optional

Required for e-commerce sites under various consumer protection laws. Should clearly state shipping costs, delivery times, and available shipping methods.

Learn more: EU E-commerce Rules
üîû Age Verification Optional

Required by COPPA (US) for sites collecting data from children under 13, and by various regulations for alcohol, tobacco, gambling, and adult content sites. Some jurisdictions require age gates before accessing certain content.

Learn more: FTC COPPA Rule UK Age Appropriate Design

‚ôø Accessibility Statement Optional

Increasingly required under ADA (US), European Accessibility Act (EU 2025), Section 508 (US Government), and similar laws worldwide. Documents accessibility features, known issues, and how users can request accommodations.

Learn more: WCAG Guidelines ADA Web Guidance EU Accessibility Act
üó∫Ô∏è Sitemap Optional

While not legally required, sitemaps improve accessibility and help users navigate the site. They're also beneficial for SEO and can help screen reader users understand site structure.

üö® Report Abuse Optional

Required by the UK Modern Slavery Act (2015) for large companies and Australia's Modern Slavery Act. Details steps taken to ensure slavery and human trafficking aren't part of the business or supply chain.

üå± Environmental / Sustainability Optional

üåê ICANN & Registry Compliance

üîç WHOIS / RDAP Lookup Optional

Domain registration data lookup services. ICANN requires registrars to provide RDAP (Registration Data Access Protocol) services, which replaced WHOIS on January 28, 2025. Essential for verifying domain ownership and contact information.

Learn more: ICANN RDAP ICANN Lookup Tool
üö´ Domain Abuse Contact Optional

Required under ICANN's Registrar Accreditation Agreement (RAA Section 3.18). Registrars must publish abuse contact information and have procedures to investigate and respond to abuse reports within two business days. Critical for DNS abuse mitigation.

Learn more: RAA Requirements ICANN Advisories
‚öñÔ∏è UDRP / Domain Disputes Optional

The Uniform Domain-Name Dispute-Resolution Policy (UDRP) is ICANN's mandatory arbitration process for domain name disputes. Provides information about how to file cybersquatting complaints through WIPO or other approved dispute resolution providers.

Learn more: ICANN UDRP Policy WIPO Domain Disputes
üè¢ Registrar Information Optional

Details about the ICANN-accredited registrar managing domain registrations. The 2023 Global Amendment to the RAA requires registrars to maintain transparency about their accreditation status and comply with ICANN's consensus policies.

Learn more: Accredited Registrars RAA 2013
üîÑ Domain Transfer Policy Optional

ICANN's Transfer Policy governs how domains move between registrars. Must explain how to obtain authorization codes (EPP/Auth codes), transfer locks, and the 60-day transfer prohibition after registration or previous transfer.

Learn more: ICANN Transfer Policy
